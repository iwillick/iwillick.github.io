Welcome!
Environmental stress is one of the major factors limiting the distribution of native and cultivated plants. My research characterizes the adaptive mechanisms that plants have evolved to tolerate or avoid injury and provide basic information that is needed to improve crop yields.

The majority of my work focuses on how plants respond to freezing -- a combination of low temperature, mechanical, dehydration, oxidative and osmotic stresses. I use an integrative physiological approach to target the critical tissues and organs responsible for freezing survival and then link mechanisms of injury to their molecular roots. My current research incorporates the natrual variation in switchgrass (Panicum virgatum L.) and monkeyflower (Mimulus spp.) populations to characterize how belowground perennating organs tolerate and avoid freezing injury. This work takes advantage of the established genomic and molecular resources developed at MSU for these systems.
